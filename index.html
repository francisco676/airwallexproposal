Questions buyers actually ask

What you're probably wondering. Plain answers.

I get it's important. But why not wait a quarter?+
You're on the road right now: Miami, New York, Tel Aviv, Cannes in June. That travel schedule is the urgency. Every corridor you fly through without content riding alongside it is a room you worked once and an audience you didn't keep. Those rooms put you in front of founders who will look you up that week, and a year-old feed answers for you when you're not there. A quarter of waiting is a quarter of that. Starting now costs you a five-minute Slack approval each week. Starting in Q3 means three more months of founders meeting an out-of-date feed. Section 03 has the full cost.
Why not just have ChatGPT or Claude do this?+
You can. Most LinkedIn content already does. Originality.ai's 2025 study of 3,368 posts from 99 influential profiles flagged 53.7% of long-form LinkedIn content as likely AI-generated. That's exactly the problem. The reason posts hitting your feed feel forgettable is they all read the same. We use AI heavily for the unsexy work (scraping competitor posts, tracking what category leaders publish, keeping your context graph fresh), and real humans on our team write every post. The system gives us category fluency on day one; the humans give you something the algorithm hasn't already seen 10,000 times this week.
Our buyers are founders and VCs. Do they actually pay attention to LinkedIn?+
They live there. Founders raising, hiring, and choosing a financial stack research the operators behind a company on LinkedIn before a call ever gets booked. VCs use it even harder; it's where deal flow and reputations move. Your own data proves it: the April announcement pulled 1,248 reactions and 229 comments, almost all of them founders and investors. The audience is already there and already responding. The gap was never the channel. It's cadence.
What if you don't want to post, or hate the drafts?+
Nothing ships without your approval. The five-minute Slack review is a feature, not a workaround. If a draft isn't right, we rewrite. If the system isn't clicking by day 30, we pause and re-scope, with no clawback fights. The 90-day window is structured so the call gets made on real data, not vibes.
Won't three Airwallex execs posting at once just compete with each other?+
The opposite, when it's run from one desk. The three-exec option puts you, Kai, and Rahul on a single content calendar with one writer coordinating it. You hold the startup lane, Kai holds cross-border and APAC infrastructure, Rahul holds the New York founder scene. Different lanes, different angles, one Airwallex narrative, so a founder who sees all three reads a company with depth rather than three people chasing the same post. Split across three separate vendors, that coordination is the first thing you lose.
What happens after 90 days?+
If it's working, we keep going (month-to-month, same rate, still no auto-renewal). If it isn't, we shake hands and you keep every post, the voice node, the context graph, and the playbook for running it in-house. The 90 days are designed so the renew/walk call gets made on real data, not vibes.
Who owns the IP, the posts, and the context graph?+
Airwallex owns everything. Every post, the full Context Graph, the voice node, the 90-day cadence playbook. Walk after the pilot and you walk with all of it. We don't gate it behind continued retainer.
